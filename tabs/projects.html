ADS-B Aircraft Tracking I embarked on a project focused on aircraft tracking in my local area, employing radio technology as the key component. By leveraging readily available public domain software, my personal computer, RTL-SDR dongle, and antennas, as well as using Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast (ADS-B) technology, I was able to capture and analyze radio signals, leading to the successful gathering of accurate locational data for individual aircraft. Search-and-Rescue Robot This was my class project for an electrical electrical engineering systems design class. The robot's objecive had been to assist with rescue operations in fire accidents or wildfires. The robot needed to auto-navigate through terrains as such and detect hot objects. The robot's parts comprised sonar sensors, a thermopile, motor control driver, multiplexer, Arduino Nano, and more. This project taught me top-down design and bottom-up implementation, as well as the need to "fail early and fail often" in the engineering process. Arduino, Controls, BeagleBoneBlue, Sensors, Image and Audio Processing
